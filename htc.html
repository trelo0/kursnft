Once again: so what is NFT?

The term "interchangeable" is used to refer to something that is not unique. For example, banknotes, company shares, sweets in a box of chocolates, and so on are not unique. This means that one such item can be replaced with a similar one without any change in value.

At the same time, there are non-interchangeable things - with them everything is exactly the opposite. They are unique or rare and cannot be replaced by another similar item. For example, an old pocket watch that your grandfather gave you is unique. This is a completely different story, because here we are talking about something irreplaceable or emotions that cannot be recreated with the help of something else. All of the above are non-interchangeable.

The same goes for Leonardo da Vinci's Mona Lisa or Vincent van Gogh's Starry Night. Of course, these works have copies, but the original exists only one. And it cannot be replaced. And now we take any of the listed items and represent it on the blockchain using a unique cryptocurrency token. This is the non-fungible NFT token. These NFTs are stored on the blockchain and serve as proof of ownership of some real or virtual asset. On top of that, being on a blockchain makes NFTs secure and immutable, so no one can change the data on them.

The ability of NFTs to validate the uniqueness of an item in a provable and secure manner can be of great benefit in many industries. For example, NFT tokens will be useful in games for representing unique in-game items, deals with unique works of art, respecting copyright in music albums, verifying ownership, and much more.

How NFT are created ?

The world of NFTs is completely different. Here it is you - the creator - who controls everything that happens with your work. There are global NFT trading platforms that will showcase your work to the world. You do not have to sign a contract with a gallery or agency to sell your work. There is no need to share profits with intermediaries either. In general, trading platforms for NFTs will even be able to provide you with a percentage of subsequent sales of your work. Accordingly, with each further transaction, you will receive a certain commission from it. In addition to this, NFT will allow you to confirm the originality of your work at any time - as well as the ownership of its owner.

Second step

Choose marketplace and register

You will need a marketplace where anyone can easily create a token on a specific blockchain and put it up for sale without any special knowledge.On many popular platforms such as OpenSea, registration can be done by simply connecting your online wallet.

Third step

Create a crypto wallet and connect it to the platform

To pay the fee for creating a token and placing it on the marketplace, you need a wallet with the corresponding cryptocurrency.

Fourth step

Creation of NFTs

Upload a file with your digital object in one of the supported formats, add its name, description and confirm the creation of the NFT. The fee payment process varies from marketplace to marketplace. Some charge a fee for creating an NFT, others for trading, and some make the buyer pay a fee.

Fifth step

Sale

Find the "Sell" button on the page with your NFT. You can choose a fixed price or put the token up for auction and choose the period when bids are accepted. Additional options such as private or pending sales are also available across platforms.

Sixth step
